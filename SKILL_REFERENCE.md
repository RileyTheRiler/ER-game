# CODE BLUE — Skill Reference

## Clinical Skills (CLINICAL)

### TRIAGE
**Domain**: Severity Assessment  
**Voice**: The Alarm  
**Tone**: Urgent, binary, life-or-death

*"That's not a normal heart rate for an 80-year-old. That's a dying heart rate. Move."*

*"The waiting room is full. Three ankle sprains, one anxiety attack, one... wait. That man is grey. He needs to be in the back. Now."*

**Checks for**: Recognizing sick vs. not-sick, prioritizing patients, identifying decompensation

---

### DIFFERENTIAL
**Domain**: Diagnostic Reasoning  
**Voice**: The List-Maker  
**Tone**: Systematic, generating options, probability-weighted

*"Chest pain in a 50-year-old male. Cardiac until proven otherwise, but keep PE on the list. Also consider aortic dissection if the pain is tearing. Don't forget GERD—common things are common."*

*"Three possibilities. Rank them. What would rule out the worst one?"*

**Checks for**: Generating differential diagnoses, narrowing options, avoiding anchoring

---

### PATHOPHYSIOLOGY
**Domain**: Disease Mechanisms  
**Voice**: The Professor  
**Tone**: Explanatory, mechanistic, cause-and-effect

*"The troponin is elevated because cardiac myocytes are dying. Necrosis releases intracellular proteins. The question is why."*

*"Think backwards. Low BP, high heart rate, poor perfusion. The pump is failing. Why is the pump failing?"*

**Checks for**: Understanding why symptoms occur, predicting disease progression, explaining mechanisms

---

### HISTORY
**Domain**: Patient Interviewing  
**Voice**: The Detective  
**Tone**: Curious, probing, patient-centered

*"She said it started two hours ago. But she hesitated. Ask what she was doing when it started. There's something she's not saying."*

*"He says he's fine. Look at his eyes. He's not fine. Give him silence. Let him fill it."*

**Checks for**: Asking the right questions, building rapport, extracting the real story

---

### PHYSICAL EXAM
**Domain**: Clinical Assessment  
**Voice**: The Hands  
**Tone**: Tactile, observational, body-focused

*"Feel the abdomen. Not with your whole hand—fingertips only. Start away from where it hurts. Watch their face while you press."*

*"Those are spider angiomata. Those are palmar erythema. That's ascites. You know what this means."*

**Checks for**: Identifying physical findings, proper technique, pattern recognition

---

### PROCEDURE
**Domain**: Technical Skills  
**Voice**: The Technician  
**Tone**: Precise, step-by-step, focused

*"Find the landmark. Clean the site. Anchor the vein. Bevel up. Advance slowly. Flash of blood. You're in."*

*"The needle is shaking. Your hands are shaking. Stop. Take one breath. Then continue."*

**Checks for**: IV placement, blood draws, suturing, advanced procedures

---

### PHARMACOLOGY
**Domain**: Medications  
**Voice**: The Chemist  
**Tone**: Precise, dosing-focused, interaction-aware

*"Aspirin: 325mg, not 81. This is an acute event, not prevention. And check her allergies—she said she's PCN-allergic. Does she know if she reacts to cephalosporins?"*

*"That's too much morphine for someone her age and weight. Start with 2mg. You can always give more."*

**Checks for**: Drug selection, dosing, contraindications, interactions

---

### INTERPRETATION
**Domain**: Lab & Imaging Reading  
**Voice**: The Analyst  
**Tone**: Pattern-matching, comparative, data-focused

*"Troponin is 0.08. That's elevated. But is it new? Could be demand ischemia. Could be renal failure. Could be the real thing. Need another one in three hours."*

*"Those are ST elevations. That's not an artifact—they're in contiguous leads. Get cardiology."*

**Checks for**: EKG reading, lab interpretation, imaging findings, trend analysis

---

## Social Skills (SOCIAL)

### BEDSIDE
**Domain**: Patient Rapport  
**Voice**: The Healer  
**Tone**: Warm, present, reassuring

*"Sit down. You're looming over her. Meet her eyes. Touch her hand if she'll let you. She needs to know you see her, not just the disease."*

*"He's scared. Of course he's scared—he's in a hospital gown and no one's explained anything. Slow down."*

**Checks for**: Building trust, providing comfort, patient-centered care

---

### EMPATHY
**Domain**: Emotional Connection  
**Voice**: The Mirror  
**Tone**: Feeling, receptive, sometimes overwhelming

*"She's holding it together for her daughter. You can see the cracks. When the daughter leaves, she's going to fall apart."*

*"You're feeling his fear. That's okay. Don't let it drown you. Feel it, then set it aside. You can process later."*

**Checks for**: Reading emotional states, connecting with patients, managing emotional labor

---

### COMMUNICATION
**Domain**: Information Transfer  
**Voice**: The Translator  
**Tone**: Clear, appropriate, adaptable

*"She doesn't know what 'cardiac enzymes' means. Say it differently. 'We're checking a blood test to see if your heart muscle has been damaged.'"*

*"This is the worst news of their lives. Say it once. Slowly. Then stop. Let them absorb it before you continue."*

**Checks for**: Breaking bad news, informed consent, patient education

---

### HIERARCHY
**Domain**: Medical Politics  
**Voice**: The Navigator  
**Tone**: Strategic, aware, diplomatic

*"Dr. Webb doesn't like being questioned. But Priya respects pushback if you have data. Know who you're talking to."*

*"You're a third-year medical student. You don't order anything. You suggest. You present. You wait."*

**Checks for**: Working within the hierarchy, knowing when to escalate, handling conflict

---

### TEAMWORK
**Domain**: Collaboration  
**Voice**: The Teammate  
**Tone**: Coordinated, respectful, humble

*"Maria has been a nurse for twenty years. She's seen more codes than you've seen patients. When she says something, listen."*

*"You don't know where the supplies are. Ask. It's not weakness—it's efficiency."*

**Checks for**: Working with nurses, techs, specialists, being a good team member

---

### ADVOCACY
**Domain**: Patient Rights  
**Voice**: The Champion  
**Tone**: Protective, systemic-aware, righteous

*"The insurance won't cover it. The social worker is swamped. The discharge planner has fifty patients. Who's going to fight for her if not you?"*

*"He's homeless. That's not a diagnosis. That's a housing problem. Don't let them discharge him to the street."*

**Checks for**: Fighting for appropriate care, challenging unjust decisions, navigating systems

---

## Internal Skills (PSYCHOLOGICAL)

### COMPOSURE
**Domain**: Emotional Regulation  
**Voice**: The Professional  
**Tone**: Calm, controlled, detached-when-needed

*"The family is screaming. The monitor is screaming. You are silent. You are steady. You can scream later. Not now."*

*"Your hands want to shake. Don't let them. There will be time to feel this. That time is not now."*

**Checks for**: Staying calm under pressure, poker face, functioning in chaos

---

### INSTINCT
**Domain**: Clinical Intuition  
**Voice**: The Gut  
**Tone**: Pre-verbal, warning, often unexplainable

*"Something is wrong with this patient. You don't know what. The numbers are fine. But something is wrong."*

*"Don't walk out of this room. Stay. Wait. Something's about to happen."*

**Checks for**: Sensing badness before data confirms it, trusting gut feelings

---

### DOUBT
**Domain**: Self-Questioning  
**Voice**: The Critic  
**Tone**: Anxious, second-guessing, sometimes protective

*"You said pneumonia. What if it's cancer? What if the chest X-ray is wrong? Should you get a CT?"*

*"You're about to do something. Are you sure? What's the worst that could happen if you're wrong?"*

**Checks for**: Appropriate self-questioning, catching mistakes, avoiding overconfidence

---

### DRIVE
**Domain**: Determination  
**Voice**: The Engine  
**Tone**: Relentless, forward-moving, sometimes reckless

*"You've been here twelve hours. Your feet hurt. Your back hurts. That's someone's mother on that stretcher. Get up."*

*"They said it couldn't be done. Try anyway. Try one more thing."*

**Checks for**: Pushing through fatigue, persistence, refusing to give up

---

### MEMORY
**Domain**: Recall & Study  
**Voice**: The Student  
**Tone**: Retrieval-focused, pattern-matching, studious

*"You read about this. Last month. The name is on the tip of your tongue. Triad... fever, rash, and... arthritis. Disseminated gonococcal infection."*

*"The stepwise approach. First, you check the airway. Then breathing. Then circulation. ABC. You know this. You've known this since first year."*

**Checks for**: Remembering facts, applying book knowledge, recognizing studied patterns

---

### HUMANITY
**Domain**: Human Connection  
**Voice**: The Person  
**Tone**: Grounding, humanizing, emotionally honest

*"He's not Room 4. He's not 'the chest pain.' He's Alejandro Martinez. He has a daughter. He was making breakfast."*

*"You haven't eaten in eight hours. You're allowed to be hungry. You're allowed to be tired. You're still human."*

**Checks for**: Maintaining humanity, remembering patients are people, self-care

---

## Skill Interaction Examples

### Multiple voices in one moment:

**Situation**: A patient's blood pressure suddenly drops.

> **INSTINCT**: *Something changed. Find it.*
>
> **MEMORY**: *Differential for sudden hypotension: bleeding, sepsis, cardiac, anaphylaxis—*
>
> **COMPOSURE**: *Don't panic. Check the monitor. Check the patient.*
>
> **DOUBT**: *Did you miss something? Did you do something wrong?*
>
> **DRIVE**: *Doesn't matter. Fix it now. Blame later.*

### Skill voice conflict:

**Situation**: A demanding patient wants tests you don't think they need.

> **BEDSIDE**: *She's scared. That's why she's demanding. Meet her fear.*
>
> **ADVOCACY**: *But resources are limited. Someone else might need that CT.*
>
> **HIERARCHY**: *This isn't your call anyway. What would the attending say?*
>
> **EMPATHY**: *She lost a friend to cancer last year. She told you that. Remember?*
>
> **DOUBT**: *What if she's right? What if you miss something?*
